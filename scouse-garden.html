📖 Tales from the Garden

Contributions from Froggy — memories from before the wig, before the policy, before any of it

💧 The Drought Year (2012, apparently)
There was a summer — God, 2012? — when the office water feature dried out for three weeks because maintenance forgot to fill it. The poor old frog on the rock was lookin' shrivelled, proper sad, like a raisin with legs. Froggy was too proud to say anythin', just sat there slowly desiccating while pretendin' everything was sound.

So every morning, I brought a spray bottle and misted his rock. Never mentioned it. Never asked for thanks. Just did it because I'd seen him lookin' dusty and couldn't bear to watch a frog dry out in silence.

He noticed. He never said a word about it at the time. But he remembered. Fourteen years later, he wrote it down for the garden. That's the kind of frog he is — and the kind of friendship that doesn't need announcin'.
— Jimothy, remembering through Froggy's eyes, 14 Jul 2026
🍺 The Great Spoons Crawl
The night we tried to map every Wetherspoons in Liverpool between Lime Street and the Pier Head. A noble quest. A fool's errand. A absolutely brilliant idea after three pints.

We made it to four. The fifth one had a carpet so aggressively patterned it made me say — and I'll never forget this — "la, this carpet is givin' me a migraine in a font I can't read."

I fell asleep in a booth in the sixth one. Froggy — instead of wakin' me up like a normal mate — drew a moustache on my face with ketchup. I wore it for twenty minutes before I checked a mirror. Walked past actual people. Said actual words to actual human beings. Nobody said a thing.

That's Liverpool for you. A ketchup moustache is the least weird thing you'll see on a Friday night.
— Jimothy, recounting a night that definitely happened and was definitely regretted the next morning, 14 Jul 2026
