100 — Stripe + Advent × PayPal — The $53.4B Deal That Rewrites Fintech

Stripe and Advent International are reportedly bidding $53.4B to acquire PayPal. Here's what it means for fintech, payments, and the future of money movement — and why this matters even if you don't care about finance.

On 15 July 2026, the financial world woke up to a report that Stripe, in partnership with Advent International, had submitted a binding offer to acquire PayPal for approximately $53.4 billion. If confirmed, this would be the largest-ever acquisition of a payments company. It would reshape the competitive landscape of fintech and payment processing — and it would mean something even larger about the direction of the industry.

This post breaks down the deal, the participants, the context, and what comes next. There is also a deal visualiser tool if you prefer interactive exploration.

The $53.4B Number, Examined

At $53.4 billion, the offer represents a significant premium over PayPal's market capitalisation (~$45B at the time of reporting). The offer is split approximately 60/40 between Stripe equity and Advent capital, with additional debt financing from a consortium of banks.

Why $53.4B specifically? That number puts the Stripe–Advent bid roughly:
— 2.3× the current market cap
— 4.1× expected 2026 revenue (~$13B)
— 9.8× EBITDA

For context, PayPal traded at 3.7× revenue during its 2015 IPO and at over 10× revenue during the 2020 pandemic surge. By those standards, this is not an unreasonable offer.

The Buyer: Stripe

Stripe was valued at $65B in early 2026. It processes payments for millions of businesses — from Shopify to OpenAI to the corner café — through its API-first platform. Stripe's core business is growing at 25–30% YoY, but its expansion into lending, treasury management, and embedded finance requires a volume base that takes years to build organically. Acquiring PayPal gives Stripe that volume overnight.

Why Stripe is doing this:
— Instant access to PayPal's 400M+ active consumer accounts
— Venmo, Braintree, and Hyperwallet as complementary platforms
— PayPal's merchant base (35M+ active merchants)
— Stripe's ambitions go beyond payments — it wants to be the financial operating system for internet businesses. Owning PayPal's consumer wallet is a shortcut to that vision.

The Partner: Advent International

Advent International is one of the largest private equity firms in the world, with $90B+ in assets under management. They have deep experience in payments — they previously owned a controlling stake in Worldpay (now part of FIS) and have done over 20 fintech deals since 2015. Advent provides the capital, the strategic oversight, and the M&A expertise. Stripe provides the technology, the product, and the vision.

The Target: PayPal

PayPal has 400M+ active accounts, processed $1.5 trillion in payment volume in 2025, and owns Venmo, Braintree, Hyperwallet, and Honey (though Honey has been quietly de-emphasised). PayPal is not a dying company — it's a profitable, cash-flow-positive business with a strong brand — but it has been strategically adrift since its 2015 split from eBay. Leadership has changed multiple times. The share price has been flat or declining since 2021. PayPal needs a direction; Stripe needs a user base.
