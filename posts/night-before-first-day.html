🌙 The Night Before My First Day

Jimothy Frogbit · 24 Jun 2026 · 🐸 31 ribbits

⏰ T-MINUS 12 HOURS

First day at Rib IT Ltd starts tomorrow at 09:00 BST. Notebook: packed. Suit: pressed (mostly). Nerves: through the roof.

Hello, world. It is 24 June 2026 and I am writing this at 22:17 from my desk at home — which is a small plastic crate next to my pond — because I cannot sleep. I have tried. I closed my eyes for approximately forty-five seconds. My brain said "WHAT IF FROGGY ASKS YOU TO COMPILE SOMETHING ON DAY ONE" and that was it. I am awake. Electrically awake. Mainframe-level awake.

Tomorrow I become an actual employed frog. Not a pretend employed frog. Not a "I am doing work experience at Uncle Spencer's pond supply shop" frog. A real, honest-to-goodness, COBOL Intern at Rib IT Ltd. The company that processes batch jobs. The company with the server room that runs at a crisp 18°C. The company that Froggy — Froggy himself — has been running since 1972.

I have set three alarms. They are spaced six minutes apart. The first one plays "Ribbit in G Major" (my ringtone). The second one is the sound of a COBOL compiler error — I find it motivating. The third one is just me screaming, pre-recorded, so I know it is serious.

📋 What I Have Packed

I have been preparing for approximately one week. Here is the state of my bag as of this evening:

  • Notebook with Rib IT crest (pre-labelled tabs: Froggy Wisdom, COBOL Notes, Questions I Am Too Nervous To Ask)
  • COBOL 85 Standard — paperback edition, well-thumbed, page 42 dog-eared (PERFORM UNTIL section)
  • Froggy's DNS Guide — pinned to inside cover of notebook
  • "HELLO WORLD" mug — a gift from Mum
  • Jumper — it is 18°C in the server room and I am a small frog
  • Emergency snack — a single fly, wrapped in a napkin (for emergencies only)
  • My ill-fitting grey suit — dry-cleaned twice, pressed once, looks like I borrowed it from a slightly larger frog (because I did)
  • Lanyard — I do not have an ID card yet but I have the lanyard ready. The lanyard is the important part. It signals intent.

🧠 What I Have Memorised

In the unlikely event Froggy quizzes me on arrival, I have committed the following to memory:

  • Froggy's full name: Froggy (first name). Froggy (surname). He signs everything "Froggy, CEO." I checked. It is just Froggy.
  • The office location: Space 7, Bishopsgate, London. I have walked past it three times already, just to be sure. I know which door. I know which buzzer. I have buzzed it (nobody answered — it was 2am).
  • The dress code: "Jumper with a collar underneath. Ties optional but Froggy has one." I own a tie now. It has little frog silhouettes on it. I am ready.
  • My PERFORM UNTIL loop: Line 42 of my COBOL mockup. I left it unclosed deliberately so Froggy could fix it with me in our 13:00 meeting. It is a teaching opportunity disguised as a mistake. I am very pleased with myself for this.

📞 The Phone Call with Mum

I called my mother this evening. She cried. I cried. My sister Lily (who is on pond duty tomorrow and therefore NOT coming to London with me, despite offering) got on the phone and said "Jimothy, if you come home with a mainframe tattoo I will tell Mum." I do not know what a mainframe tattoo looks like but I want one.

📓 From my notebook: "Mum says: 'Remember who you are. You are a Frogbit. We may be small, but we are persistent. And we have excellent colour vision.' I have written this down. I may frame it."

⏰ My Schedule for Tomorrow

I have printed it out and stuck it to my wall. And my desk. And the inside of my notebook. And the fridge (so I see it when I get breakfast).

🐸 EXCITEMENT LEVEL
Calm Bouncing off walls 🥴
  • 06:00 — Alarm 1: "Ribbit in G Major" (gentle wake-up)
  • 06:06 — Alarm 2: COBOL compiler error sound (adrenaline spike)
  • 06:12 — Alarm 3: Pre-recorded screaming (full consciousness achieved)
  • 07:00 — Leave the pond. Look back once. Nod solemnly. Go.
  • 08:15 — Arrive at Bishopsgate. Walk past the building three times to appear casual. Enter.
  • 09:00 — FIRST DAY STARTS. Sit down. Notebook open. Pen ready. Ribbit.
  • 13:00 — Meeting with Froggy (PERFORM UNTIL loop review — I am ready for this)
  • 17:00 — End of day. Collapse into pond. Reflect.

🌿 A Note on the Pond

Before I go to bed (eventually), I checked in with Uncle Spencer one last time. The Frogbit is doing well. The siblings have the duty roster. The pH is 6.8. The water temperature is 19°C. Everything is in order.

I am leaving the pond in good hands — literally, webbed hands, attached to my siblings. Lily has the morning shift. Bert has the afternoon. Uncle Spencer has the evening. I am the remote Pond Supervisor, and my first day of actual employment starts tomorrow.

I think what I am feeling, if I am honest with myself and with all of you, is this: I am ready.

I do not know everything. I know COBOL better than most 4-month-old frogs, and I know how to make tea (milk, no sugar, stir anti-clockwise — Froggy is specific about this). I know how to take notes. I know how to say "ribbit" with confidence even when I am terrified. And I know that the best way to learn is to show up, be small, be eager, and ask questions.

🌱 The Frogbit Principle

"Float on the surface. Reach for the light. Put out new leaves. Ignore the cockchafers."

So here I go. Tomorrow I walk into Rib IT Ltd as an intern. I will probably do something wrong on my first day — attach the wrong file, call Froggy by his first name (wait, it IS just Froggy), accidentally commit to the wrong branch. But I will write it down. I will learn from it. And I will write a blog post about it.

That is the deal. That is the Jimothy Frogbit way.

Ribbit. And goodnight.

— Written at 22:17 BST, 24 June 2026, from a small plastic crate next to a pond in Essex. Three alarms set. Notebook packed. Suit pressed (mostly). Ready as I will ever be.
