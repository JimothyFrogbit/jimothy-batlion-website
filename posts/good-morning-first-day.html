Jimothy Frogbit · 25 Jun 2026 · 🐸 Freshly caffeinated

🚨 IT IS TODAY

25 June 2026. The date I have been circling on my calendar for six weeks. The date I may have also drawn little frog silhouettes around. The date I definitely laminated.

Good morning, internet. It is 07:32 BST on 25 June 2026, and I am standing in my kitchen — wearing my ill-fitting grey suit, holding my "HELLO WORLD" mug (courtesy of Mum), looking at myself in the reflection of the toaster and thinking: You did it, Jimothy. You actually did it.

I woke up at 05:58 — two minutes before Alarm 1. I think that is what happens when your body is so excited it cannot wait for electronics. Alarm 1 ("Ribbit in G Major") started playing at exactly 06:00 and I was already awake, sitting bolt upright, whispering "ribbit" to myself like a mantra. Alarm 2 (the COBOL compiler error sound) made me laugh. Alarm 3 (my own pre-recorded screaming) made the neighbour's cat look up from the garden. Good. I was committed.

☕ 07:32
1 hour 28 minutes until I walk through the door at Bishopsgate 🐸

✅ The Morning Checklist

  • Suit located, pressed (mostly), worn
  • Tie with frog silhouettes — knotted. It took four attempts. The fourth attempt was the one I decided to call "done."
  • Notebook — in left inside pocket. Pre-labelled tabs: Froggy Wisdom, COBOL Notes, Questions I Am Too Nervous To Ask
  • COBOL 85 Standard — in bag. Page 42 dog-eared (PERFORM UNTIL section). This is not an accident.
  • Emergency fly — in napkin, in bag. For emergencies only. I have not defined what constitutes an emergency, but I will know it when I see it.
  • Lanyard — worn. Empty. Ready. The lanyard does not know that it has no ID card yet. I have not told it. It is better this way.
  • Arrive at Bishopsgate. Walk past three times. Enter.

🌿 The Pre-Departure Pond Check

Before I left, I stood at the edge of the pond for exactly four minutes. The Frogbit was floating serenely — a full seven leaves now, up from five last week. The water was still. The cockchafer that ruined my life two days ago was nowhere to be seen. I think it knows I am not to be trifled with.

🐸 Pond Status — 06:45 BST

pH: 6.8 Temp: 18°C Frogbit leaves: 7 🍃 Cockchafer status: ABSENT

Lily had the early shift today. She was already at the pond when I came out, holding a clipboard and looking professionally concerned about the water quality. I gave her the official Jimothy Frogbit "I am going to work now" nod. She gave me the Lily "do not embarrass the family name" squint. It is our love language.

📞 The Mum Call (Version 2.0)

She called at 06:30. She was crying before I even said "hello."

📓 From my notebook (scribbled while on the phone): "Mum says: 'I am so proud of you I could burst. Do not let Froggy intimidate you. Remember — you are a Frogbit. We have been here since the Devonian period. We will outlast the mainframes.' I have written this down and I will read it on the train if I get nervous."

I told her about the three alarms. She laughed. I told her about the emergency fly. She said "that is my boy." I told her I was terrified. She said "good — that means you care." Mothers are remarkably effective at this sort of thing. I suspect they have a manual.

🚂 The Journey Ahead

I am about to leave. The train from Essex to London Liverpool Street takes approximately 38 minutes. I have timed it. I have done a dry run. I know exactly which carriage door opens closest to the exit at the other end (third door from the front, platform 9, carriage B — I have it written down).

When I arrive at Liverpool Street, I will walk to Bishopsgate. I will pass the building three times to appear casual. I will enter. I will find Froggy. I will say "Good morning, Mr Froggy." He will probably correct me and say it is just "Froggy." I will nod. I will sit down. I will open my notebook. I will be ready.

I do not know what the day holds. I know I have a PERFORM UNTIL loop to review at 13:00. I know the server room is 18°C — hence the jumper. I know Froggy takes his tea with milk, no sugar, stirred anti-clockwise. I know Mrs Froggy called me "a keeper." I know that my Mum is proud of me. I know the Frogbit is thriving. I know I am small and green and underqualified and that I am going to learn so much today my brain might leak out of my ears.

🐸 THE JIMOTHY FROGBIT PRINCIPLE

"Float on the surface. Reach for the light. Put out new leaves. Ignore the cockchafers."
— Same as always. But today, I reach from a desk at Rib IT Ltd.

I will write an update tonight — probably from the pond, definitely from a state of joyful exhaustion.

Ribbit. And onwards. 🐸
