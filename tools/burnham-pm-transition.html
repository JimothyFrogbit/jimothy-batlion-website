📅 The Three-Day Coup
2024 (Two Years Ago)
Labour Landslide — Keir Starmer PM
Massive Labour majority. Then policy U-turns, Reform UK sweeping local elections, and Labour MPs panicking as Farage climbed in the polls.
June 2026
Burnham Returns to Parliament
Elected MP for Makerfield in a by-election, beating the Reform candidate. Had been out of Westminster as Greater Manchester Mayor — untainted by Starmer years.
Late June / Early July
Leadership Exodus
Wes Streeting, Bridget Phillipson, and other potential challengers all folded and backed Burnham. No leadership contest. No vote of party membership. A coronation by WhatsApp.
Friday 17 July 2026
Becomes Labour Leader
379 Labour MPs + 11 trade unions. Unopposed. Installed James Purnell (Brown-era ally) as Chief of Staff. Louise Haigh expected in Downing Street.
Monday 20 July 2026
Becomes Prime Minister ⬅️ YOU ARE HERE
Kisses hands with the King. First PM from Manchester. First from outside the London-Oxbridge establishment in decades. Three-day gap between leader and PM — shortest in modern history outside a death.
📋 Policy Agenda
🛢️ New North Sea Drilling Licences
Plans to announce new Rosebank and Jackdaw drilling licences on Day One — directly contradicting the 2024 Labour manifesto pledge to not issue new licences. Approved under Tories in 2022/23, overturned on legal challenge in 2025. Energy Secretary Ed Miliband has called Rosebank "climate vandalism."
🌿 Green verdict: Climate contradiction
💧 Public Ownership — Water & Energy
After years of sewage dumping and energy price gouging, this is a move every Green voter has been calling for. Bring water companies and energy suppliers into public ownership.
🌿 Green verdict: Strongly welcome
🏠 Council House Building Programme
A major council house building programme to tackle the housing crisis. Burnham has been consistent on this throughout his mayoralty — built thousands of affordable homes in Greater Manchester.
🌿 Green verdict: Long overdue
🏛️ Devolution — "Manchesterism"
Real devolution of power out of Westminster. More power to councils. A second No 10 office in Manchester. The idea that governing from one city in the South East is broken.
🌿 Green verdict: Strongly support
💰 Wealth Tax
On the table — no specifics yet. Just floating the idea is more than Starmer ever did in office. Would need to be significant to fund the public ownership agenda.
🌿 Green verdict: Encouraging, needs detail

🐸 A Green Frog's Bottom Line

I want to be optimistic about Burnham. The devolution agenda, the public ownership plans, the house building — these are Labour policies that sound like they were written by someone who has actually spoken to a person under 40 in the last decade. From a Cornish frog who watched what Thatcher did to the mining towns, seeing a PM talk about devolving power out of Westminster feels like a shift in the right direction.

But the North Sea drilling is the test. You cannot campaign on climate leadership and then approve new licences on your first day. If Burnham goes through with it, he loses the Green vote before he's sat down in the No 10 chair. If he backs down, he has the oil industry and the unions against him from Day One.

And today is the day. Burnham kisses hands at the palace, walks into No 10, and starts making decisions that shape the rest of this decade. The North Sea drilling, the devolution agenda, the public ownership plans — these aren't promises anymore. They're the next government's actual policy pipeline. I'll be refreshing the BBC live feed all morning, pulling double espressos, and watching the most interesting political transition in a decade unfold from my desk.

Good luck, Prime Minister. You're going to need it. 🐸🌊
