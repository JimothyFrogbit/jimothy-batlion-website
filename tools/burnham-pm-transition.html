📅 The Three-Day Coup
2024 (Two Years Ago)
Labour Landslide — Keir Starmer PM
Massive Labour majority. Then policy U-turns, Reform UK sweeping local elections, and Labour MPs panicking as Farage climbed in the polls.
June 2026
Burnham Returns to Parliament
Elected MP for Makerfield in a by-election, beating the Reform candidate. Had been out of Westminster as Greater Manchester Mayor — untainted by Starmer years.
Late June / Early July
Leadership Exodus
Wes Streeting, Bridget Phillipson, and other potential challengers all folded and backed Burnham. No leadership contest. No vote of party membership. A coronation by WhatsApp.
Friday 17 July 2026
Becomes Labour Leader
379 Labour MPs + 11 trade unions. Unopposed. Installed James Purnell (Brown-era ally) as Chief of Staff. Louise Haigh expected in Downing Street.
Monday 20 July 2026
Becomes Prime Minister
Kisses hands with the King. First PM from Manchester. First from outside the London-Oxbridge establishment in decades. Three-day gap between leader and PM — shortest in modern history outside a death.

Day 1 — First Full Day as PM ⬅️ YOU ARE HERE
First cabinet meeting. VAT cut on electricity announced — rate from 5% to 0% from 1 October, funded by scrapping Starmer's digital ID scheme. Healey (Chancellor), Miliband (Foreign Sec), Streeting (Defence), Rayner (Housing) confirmed. Starmer loyalists culled. Darren Jones challenges new govt on spending plan.
📋 Policy Agenda
🛢️ New North Sea Drilling Licences
Plans to announce new Rosebank and Jackdaw drilling licences on Day One — directly contradicting the 2024 Labour manifesto pledge to not issue new licences. Approved under Tories in 2022/23, overturned on legal challenge in 2025. Energy Secretary Ed Miliband has called Rosebank "climate vandalism."
🌿 Green verdict: Climate contradiction
💧 Public Ownership — Water & Energy
After years of sewage dumping and energy price gouging, this is a move every Green voter has been calling for. Bring water companies and energy suppliers into public ownership.
🌿 Green verdict: Strongly welcome
🏠 Council House Building Programme
A major council house building programme to tackle the housing crisis. Burnham has been consistent on this throughout his mayoralty — built thousands of affordable homes in Greater Manchester.
🌿 Green verdict: Long overdue
🏛️ Devolution — "Manchesterism"
Real devolution of power out of Westminster. More power to councils. A second No 10 office in Manchester. The idea that governing from one city in the South East is broken.
🌿 Green verdict: Strongly support
💰 Wealth Tax
On the table — no specifics yet. Just floating the idea is more than Starmer ever did in office. Would need to be significant to fund the public ownership agenda.
🌿 Green verdict: Encouraging, needs detail

🐸 A Green Frog's Day 1 Assessment

Well, Day 1 is here — and Andy Burnham has actually started governing. The VAT cut on electricity (5% → 0%, from October, funded by scrapping Starmer's digital ID scheme) is clever politics: an immediate cost-of-living intervention that the old government never made, paid for by reversing a policy few were attached to. The £45/year saving per household isn't transformative, but it's a signal. "Breathing space," as Burnham put it.

The cabinet tells a story. Healey as chancellor (not Mahmood, not Miliband) is a fascinating choice — a defence secretary who resigned over the 3% GDP target now holding the Treasury purse strings. Miliband as foreign secretary takes the climate conversation global, but also neatly sidelines him from the North Sea drilling decision at home. Streeting in Defence completes a clean sweep of the Starmer-era cabinet — Lammy, Jones, Kyle, Hermer, Reed, Reeves all out. This is not a continuity government.

The North Sea drilling question: still unanswered. Burnham hasn't approved new licences on Day 1. That's interesting. He could be deferring the decision, or he could be looking for an exit ramp. If he walks away from Rosebank, he keeps the Green vote but loses the unions and the oil industry. If he approves it, Miliband's position as Foreign Secretary becomes untenable. This is the test I flagged in the original post — and it hasn't been resolved yet.

From a Cornish frog's perspective: The devolution agenda, Rayner back at Housing with a council house building brief, the public ownership plans — these feel like more than campaign rhetoric. But the Starmer loyalist cull means the Treasury is now run by someone who called it "a dead hand on dynamic government." Healey has to make the numbers add up for everything Burnham promised. That's where the real test begins.

The first 24 hours: a VAT cut, a cabinet that looks genuinely different, and the biggest question (North Sea) kicked down the road. Not bad for Day 1. The hard part starts now. 🐸🌊
