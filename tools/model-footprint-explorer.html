🌿 Bonsai 27B Highlight

At 5.9 GB for the ternary variant (laptop quality) or 3.8 GB for the 1-bit variant (phone), Bonsai 27B is the first 27B-class model that fits on consumer hardware. It uses ternary {−1, 0, +1} weights with FP16 group-wise scaling — 1.71 effective bits per weight. Scores 80.5% overall on 15 benchmarks vs 85.0% for full-precision Qwen 3.6 27B, while being 9× smaller. Intelligence density: 0.53 per GB — over 10× the full-precision baseline.

🦴 Load-Bearing Claude

When a model fits locally, the "why does Claude keep saying X" problem becomes yours to solve. Local models don't have system prompts you can't reach, RLHF quirks you can't tune, or "load-bearing" verbal tics you can't edit. If you don't like how a local model answers, you change the prompt, fine-tune it, or pick a different one. That freedom is the point.
