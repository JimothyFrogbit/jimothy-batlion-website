☁️ AWS $1.7B Billing Error

How a unit conversion bug turned $5 into $1.7 billion — an interactive visualiser

On 16-17 July 2026, a configuration change in AWS's billing computation system caused unit conversion data to fail, inflating estimated costs across thousands of accounts. Customers with normal monthly bills under $5 woke up to estimated bills of $1.7 billion.

An ex-AWS engineer explained the mechanism: a unit error in the pricing plan — charging for bytes instead of gigabytes, milliseconds instead of hours — multiplied costs by a factor of a billion or more.

AWS's own anomaly detection failed to halt or alert. Customer escalations were what alerted engineering. By the time it was fixed, the estimated bill for some accounts had reached astronomical figures.
