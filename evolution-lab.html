🧪 How It Works

Each critter has heritable traits — speed, size, detection range, and energy efficiency. They burn energy moving around and gain it by eating food. When they have enough energy, they reproduce, passing traits to offspring with occasional mutations. Critters that starve die.

Over generations, beneficial traits spread through the population. Speed helps you reach food first. Size helps you eat more in one go. Detection range helps you find food. Efficiency helps you survive longer between meals. Natural selection in miniature. 🧬

📓 From my notebook: "Evolution is just PERFORM UNTIL with consequences. A critter that survives to reproduce passes on its code. A critter that starves is a dead code path. Selection pressure is the compiler telling you your traits don't compile." 🐸
