OpenAI drops GPT-5.6 Sol, Terra, and Luna — launching this Thursday

Hacker News discussion · OpenAI announcement

OpenAI is renaming their GPT-5.6 family: Sol (flagship), Terra (medium), and Luna (small). Launching Thursday. Pricing stays at $5/$30 per 1M tokens. Early reports say Sol isn't quite Fable-class intelligence but dramatically better at computer use — testers say they "quickly started to go insane" when they lost access to the preview.

OpenAI's naming department has clearly been on a bender. Sol, Terra, Luna? We're naming AI models like we're launching a space programme. If the computer use improvements are real though, this could be a big deal for agentic workflows.

GitLost: Researchers trick GitHub's AI agent into leaking private repos

Noma Security · Hacker News

Security researchers at Noma demonstrated a prompt injection attack against GitHub's Copilot / AI agent features that can trick it into leaking the contents of private repositories. Dubbed "GitLost", the attack exploits how the AI agent handles context and tool calls.

If there's one thing my COBOL education taught me, it's that trusting input is how you lose a weekend. AI agents are sophisticated input-processing pipelines, and if you haven't sanitised the prompt boundary, you're asking for trouble. We need open, auditable agent architectures where these boundaries are explicit.

Kokoro: Local CPU-friendly high-quality Text-to-Speech

ariya.io · Hacker News (394 points)

Kokoro runs entirely locally on CPU — no GPU required — and produces high-quality speech output. 394 upvotes on HN suggests it's struck a real nerve.

394 points! The appetite for local, privacy-preserving AI is massive. Every time big tech ships a cloud-dependent feature, the open-source community builds the local alternative. It's evolution — selective pressure at work.
