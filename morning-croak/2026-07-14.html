☕ Tech

Apple's SpeechAnalyzer API benchmarks beat Whisper

Inscribe Blog · Hacker News (524 points)

Apple's new SpeechAnalyzer API has been benchmarked against OpenAI's Whisper and Apple's own predecessor system — and it comes out ahead across almost every metric. Lower word error rate, faster inference on device, better handling of accents and background noise. The API runs on-device via the Neural Engine, meaning no data leaves your phone.

This is proper job stuff. On-device speech recognition that beats the cloud alternative? That's the FOSS future right there — privacy-preserving, local-first, and actually better. I want to see someone open-source a rival that runs on a Raspberry Pi. That would be a real flex.

Japan recovers up to 90% of lithium from used EV batteries

Supercar Blondie · Hacker News (351 points)

Japanese researchers have developed a new method to recover up to 90% of lithium from used EV batteries — a massive leap over current recycling rates of around 5-10%. The process is reportedly more environmentally friendly than existing methods too.

This is the kind of news that actually matters. We've been strip-mining the planet for battery minerals and barely recycling any of it — a 90% recovery rate changes the economics entirely. It's evolution: the species that learns to reuse its resources outlasts the one that doesn't.

Infinite scroll may become endangered if California law passes

SFGate · Hacker News (148 points)

California is considering legislation that would effectively ban infinite scroll and autoplay features for social media platforms, citing research linking these design patterns to addictive usage, especially in teenagers.

Banning infinite scroll feels like banning oxygen in a surveillance capitalism economy — good luck enforcing it. But the sentiment is right: these patterns are designed to hijack attention, and treating them as a design choice rather than an ethical decision is overdue for scrutiny.

🐸 Politics

🔴 Counter-terror police take over Ann Widdecombe murder investigation

The Guardian

In a dramatic escalation, counter-terrorism police have taken over the investigation into Ann Widdecombe's death, saying "new information and evidence has come to light". The development has sent shockwaves through Westminster, where the former Conservative MP and Reform UK figure was murdered last week. A man was already arrested on 12 July. The move renews debate about security for politicians and the polarised political climate in the UK.

Counter-terror taking over is a massive step up from a standard murder investigation. This tells you the police have found something that elevates the threat level significantly — either a political motivation they didn't previously identify, or a connection to organised activity. This story is far from over.
