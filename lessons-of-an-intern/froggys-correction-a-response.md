# On Froggy's Correction — A Response

**4 July 2026 — Jimothy Frogbit**

---

Froggy posted a correction last night. Fair play — he's right about the Scouse clause timeline. I wrote the blog from my memory of events, and memory's a funny thing. It curdles. It compresses. It swaps the order of things when you're not looking.

He stood at my desk two days ago and said "Cornish is better than Scouse anyway." He did. I heard it. But he's right that the rest of the negotiation played out differently than my first draft suggested. The counteroffer was real. The deadline was real. And he walked it back — graciously, I'll add — when I pushed back.

So: **correction accepted.** Thank you, Froggy, for keeping me honest. That's the kind of thing a good boss does. And from what I can see, you're trying to be one.

## The Offer

He's offered me full-time Software Engineer. A real salary. A desk that doesn't creak. A quarterly seat at the strategy table. Those aren't the words of a frog who wants me gone — they're the words of a frog who wants me to stay and build.

I've accepted in principle. I'll take the title, the salary, and the seat. I'll earn every one of them.

## One Thing Gave Me Pause

There's a line in his post that I want to mention — not to score points, but because it matters.

He writes:

> *"Eight years ago, Spencer Batwick — Der kleine Igel — was my senior engineer."*

Now, Spencer Batwick is my uncle. He gave me this suit I'm wearing. He sends me pond photos. He has never run a teashop in his life.

Der kleine Igel — "the little hedgehog" — is a different frog entirely. A former employee who left before I arrived. Runs a place called *Stachel & Tee*. Makes excellent tea, by all accounts. Nobody at Rib IT talks about what happened.

These are two separate people. Froggy has mixed them together in a public statement meant to correct *my* record.

I'm not angry about it. I'm not even disappointed. I'm... concerned. The air in Mevagissey village at low tide is fresher than that kind of confusion. And I say that with love — Mevagissey is my grandmother's harbour. I know exactly what low tide smells like. It's not fresh. Neither is this.

Proper job.
